How to Properly Attend a Nevada Basketball or Football game

Life hacks to attend a University of Nevada Basketball or Football game

Assembly time: Years of experience
Helpfulness rating: 100% Helpful

Are you in search of life hacks to improve your experience when attending a Nevada Basketball or Football game? Long lines, packed parking lots, expensive food, expensive beer, and other burdens that entice you to watch the game at home. NONSENSE! Follow this guide to learn tips and tricks that'll save you time, money, and embarrasment as a live game noob.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Bottle
  • Cardboard
  • Hand pump (bicycle)
  • Cork (vintage wines)
  • Duct Tape
  • Water

  1. Learn about Nevada Wolf Pack History.

    I know you may be new here and unfamiliar with Nevada's storied history. Don't worry. I got you . You wouldn't want to make a new friend at a game and accidentally reveal your ignorance. Deep familiarity with these two moments will be more than enough to help you act like a proper Nevada sports nut!

  2. Park Farrrrr Awayyyyy From the Venue

    Do you see these two venues? Do you know they both hold thousands of people? Do you know what happens when thousands of people all leave one place at the same time? CHAOS. Park far away in close neighborhoods, so when the mass exodus commences, all you have to do is go for a little walk then you're long gone! Plus, the walk will help burn off the extra calories you amassed slamming beers at the game (responsibly, of course). Knock out two birds with one stone!

    Mackay Stadium and downtown Reno

  3. Complete Your Pre-game Rituals.

    Anytime before the game begins, you must complete your pre-game rituals. This step can be first or tenth, as long as it happens before the game commences. I slot my pre-game ritual right here because mine typically happens during my long walk to the game. My ritual includes slamming three or four beers with friends and housing some food, preferably Port of Subs. The expedited intake is strategic, we don't want to spend too much money at the game, so it's wise to get your mind right before entering the venue.

    "

  4. Stuff Your Pockets with "Forbidden Goods."

    Now, this step may not be for everybody. If you're uncomfortable sneaking a few items into the venue, I grant you my benevolent permission to skip this step without judgment. Please don't let me corrupt your sensibilities. Due to the previously mentioned goal of saving money, I recommend you sneak in a few cheap dollar liquor shots from the corner store (if you're of age, of course) and a few pieces of candy. I like sour skittles. This step is recommended because stadium fare is expenisve and we're cheapskates, right? We try and save money at every turn. WARNING: women's purses are checked, so don't pack that contraband in your bag. (*DISCLAIMER: this list is for entertainment purposes only, Charles Riggs and Charles Riggs affiliates do not condone illegal activity)

  5. Find Your Seat. Settle in. Enjoy the Game.

    Alright, young padawan. You're finally in the venue with your pre-game rituals completed and contraband in hand. Now what? Find your seat. If you're having trouble, there is a gaggle of stadium personnel prepared to help guide you. That's what they're here for! Next, you have to settle in. The process of "settling in" includes that $20 bill I said you'd need initially. You can't sneak in everything! Use your $20 however you see fit. I like to buy two 8$ beers and more candy. My friend's girlfriend always gets a caramel apple coated in nuts. Find your caramel apple. After you're settled, you can actually do what you came here to do, WATCH THE GAME! You don't have to study it, but please don't bury your head in your phone for most of the game.

  6. In-game Hacks

    Now that the game is playing, there isn't much else to do besides sit back, relax, and watch the Wolf Pack kick some butt, preferably UNLV. But, there are a couple of tips that can increase your and your party's experience attending a major Nevada Wolf Pack sporting event:

    1. Plan for long lines at the bathroom if you go at halftime. I recommend going to the bathroom a few minutes before or after halftime because, during halftime, there is a log jam.
    2. Save your ticket. Every ticket has a kick-ass food deal connected to a local eatery. So don't lose it.
    3. If you brought some kiddos, seek out Nevada's mascots. We have three, and they love to liven up the atmosphere and take pictures with kids. Take advantage! Plus, it's an easy way to punt the brats away for ten minutes. I doubt they'll get kidnapped!

    Wolfie, Alfie, and Luna

  7. Watch the Game Until the Very End and Laugh at Parking Garage Suckers

    Use common sense here. I'm not asking you to torture yourself and watch the Pack team get blown out. I'm asking you to stay until the game is over. Too often, fair-weather fans leave a close game to "BeAt thE TRafFic." UNPREPARED LOSERS! Remember, we're diligent, we planned for this. The whole venue could leave at once, and your exodus will be a breeze. You know why? Because you parked farrrr awayyyyy and not in the parking garage.

  8. Use Your Ticket Deal on the Way Home

    The games over, you have a designated driver, and you're hungry. No worries, use the ticket stub you save to hit up your local haunt and grab a bite. When fans last attended games, Two seasons ago, a game stub would get you a free order of chili cheese fries from Del Taco if the Men's Basketball team made a three or the Football team scored more than 21 points. And we're talking about the Wolf Pack, so that's a guarantee. As you inhale your dish, feel pride. You just attended a Nevada sporting event in the most efficient way possible. You're now officially a Wolf Pack fan. Chris Ault would be proud.
